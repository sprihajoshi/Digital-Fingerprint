On the afternoon of Friday 21 November 2025, someone rang the victim’s doorbell. A woman claimed to be from the police and said she had come round because the victim’s bank card had been misused. She was let in in good faith. Shortly afterwards, a man rang the doorbell, claiming to be a detective. Using smooth talk, the man and the woman managed to persuade the elderly victim to hand over not only her bank card and PIN, but also a substantial amount of cash. Not long after, the suspect seen on CCTV at a supermarket on Cassandraplein withdrew money using the stolen bank card. Together with the stolen cash, the victim lost nearly €2,000. The man can be described as a young man aged 15-18 years, with dark skin, round face with bulbous nose, frizzy afro hair and stocky built. Last seen in a black puffer jacket from Calvin Klein, black tracksuit bottoms and black shoes with white soles. He was also carrying a white iPhone. Fortunately, shortly after the fake police officers had left, she had already contacted a family member, who ensured that the bank card was blocked. This prevented the same suspect from spending €1,300 at a large electronics store.
